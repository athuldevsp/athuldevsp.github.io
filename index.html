01/ Research Statement & Profile

I am an experimental particle physicist working at the boundary of subatomic physics, collider analysis, and deep learning algorithms.

Following the discovery of the Higgs boson in 2012, my primary research focus centers on measuring its properties. Specifically, I study the Higgs trilinear self-coupling—a critical parameter providing direct insights into the shape of the Higgs potential. This coupling is probed at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) through Higgs boson pair production ($hh \rightarrow b\bar{b}\tau^-\tau^+$ decay channel).

For my Master's and PhD, I have optimized trigger chains using combinations of Python and C++ data analysis pipelines. I analyze Monte Carlo samples and Enhanced Bias datasets, optimizing variables like transverse momentum ($p_T$), Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) tau identifiers, and jet-particle angular separation ($\Delta R$) to enhance signal-to-background efficiency. My analyses leverage Graph Neural Networks (GNN) and high-performance computing resources via the ATLAS grid network.
